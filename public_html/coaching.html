Tournaments

1st Panathinaikos World Basketball Youth Cup

The academy team will be participating in the 1st Panathinaikos World Basketball Youth Cup. The event will take place on 4-10 July 2016 in the historical indoor sports hall “Pavlos Giannakopoulos” in Athens.

Venue

The historical indoor sports hall “Pavlos Giannakopoulos” is located under the east curve of “Apostolos Nikolaidis” Stadium, home of Panathinaikos FC and the oldest football stadium in Greece still active. It is situated in the Ambelokipi district of Athens, east of the Lycabettus Hill and on Alexandras Avenue. “Pavlos Giannakopoulos” venue was the first indoor hall in Greece with a capacity of 1.500 and it is famous for the hot atmosphere Panathinaikos fans create in it.

Panathinaikos obtained 14 Greek Championships from 1961 to 1984 in this venue and used it for home games until 1992. Famous Greek and Foreign Players have played under its roof. It is the traditional home of Panathinaikos Athletic Club academies.

Date, Age Groups & Format

Date:
The Cup will start on Monday 4th of July 10:00am and end on Sunday 10th of July at 14:00pm.

Age Groups:
There will be 2 different age groups, one for players born on 2001-2002 and the other for players born on 2003-2004. Younger players will be allowed, older players only upon discussion.

Format:
There will be 1 group for each age category. Every team will compete with another once, to get the standings of the group. All the teams will advance and form new groups according to their final position, for example: For a group of 8 teams, teams 1-4 will form a new group and teams 5-8 another. Everyone will play against each other once to get the final standings of the tournament and the winner of each age category.

Benefits:

  • Every team will play at least 6 official matches
  • The games will be professionally organized with official referees and scorekeepers
  • All participants will get: The tournament t-shirt, a small Panathinaikos banner certificate of participation, medal
  • Post event video
  • Cups for the winners
  • Scouting of young players
